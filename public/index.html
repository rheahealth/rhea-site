The Rhea Health panel screens for over 80 serious genetic conditions, including spinal muscular atrophy (SMA), cystic fibrosis, fragile X syndrome, thalassemia, and other high-impact recessive disorders. These conditions were selected based on clinical severity, carrier frequency, and the actionability of results for family planning.

A false negative means the test reports no carrier status even though a pathogenic variant is present. No screening test detects every possible variant, so a small residual risk remains after a negative result. Our panel is designed to capture the most clinically significant and common variants for each condition. A certified genetic counselor will explain your residual risk as part of your results review.
