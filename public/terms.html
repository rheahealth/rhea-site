4. Medical Disclaimer

Please read this section carefully.

Rhea Health's carrier screening is a screening service — it is not a diagnostic test and does not constitute medical advice, a medical diagnosis, or a clinical assessment.

A "carrier" result means you carry one variant copy of a gene associated with a particular condition. Most carriers are healthy and do not develop the condition themselves; the clinical significance relates primarily to reproductive planning when both partners carry variants for the same condition.

Limitations of screening:

- Our panel screens for approximately 80 conditions but does not cover all known genetic conditions or all variants within screened genes. A negative result does not eliminate the possibility of being a carrier for conditions not included in the panel, or for variants not detectable by our methodology.
- No screening test achieves 100% sensitivity or specificity. Results should be interpreted in the context of your personal and family history.
- Results are not intended to predict, diagnose, or rule out any medical condition in yourself or a future child.

What to do with your results:

You should discuss your screening report with a qualified healthcare professional — such as your obstetrician, family physician, or a certified genetic counsellor — before making any reproductive or clinical decisions.
